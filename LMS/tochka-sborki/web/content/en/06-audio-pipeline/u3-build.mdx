<Phase type="activation">

You have your pipeline design from the last unit — a sketch of how the steps connect.

Don't look at it as a diagram. **Picture it as plumbing:** every connection is a fitting the flow has to pass through. Where pipes meet tight, water runs clean. Where they're joined in a hurry — it drips.

Run each joint of your design in your head: which one honestly looks like a temporary clamp held together with tape? That's the fitting to reinforce before you turn the water on.

</Phase>

<Phase type="reflection">

Picture an editor at the cutting table: hundreds of clips in front of them, and the goal isn't to make a perfect film — it's to **assemble a first rough cut** so they can see where the story falls apart.

No great edit starts from a first take. First comes the rough cut: everything in order, even if it's uneven. Then the director watches to see what grips and what sags.

Your pipeline today is the rough cut. Notice the one spot that sagged on the first pass — that's exactly where the second version gets its upgrade.

</Phase>

<Phase type="concept">

## Full pipeline: URL → Insights

This is a working prompt you can use right now:

```
You are an analytical agent. Your job is to extract maximum value from content.

URL to analyze: [paste link]

Tasks:
1. Read the page fully
2. Pull out 5–7 actionable ideas (concrete, not generic phrases)
3. Identify 5–7 topics for further research
4. Find 2–3 best quotes or facts

Output format:
## Key ideas
[list with explanations]

## Topics to research
[list with short context]

## Worth remembering
[quotes or facts]

Save the result to a file ideas.md
```

## Extended pipeline: multiple sources

You can analyze several URLs in parallel:

```
Analyze three articles on the topic [topic]:
1. [URL 1]
2. [URL 2]
3. [URL 3]

For each: 3 ideas + 1 best quote.
Then: find what they have in common and where they contradict.
Save to research-[topic].md
```

</Phase>
