<Phase type="activation">

Think of a task you recently handed to AI — say, "write me an Instagram post."

Don't dissect the request. **Picture it as the turning of seasons.** Winter is a command with no context: a bare branch, no leaf, no fruit. Spring is the first hint of a topic. Summer is full context — audience, goal, tone, length. Autumn is iteration, refinement.

Run it in your head: which season was your request in? What hadn't bloomed in it yet?

</Phase>

<Phase type="reflection">

Now shift the frame: not seasons — **the changing of the guard.** One sentry stands exactly as long as ordered and does precisely what's in the manual. The other was told *why* they're posted here — and sees the perimeter differently.

Hold this in your head: which of your tasks is AI currently "standing guard" on without knowing why? Where are you issuing an order instead of passing along the meaning?

**The more "why" in a prompt — the less guessing in the answer.**

</Phase>

<Phase type="concept">

## Four shifts in working with AI agents

This isn't "AI doing it instead of you" — these are **four ways of co-working**: different facets of how you and the agent think and build together. There's no single right one — each fits a different task.

### 1️⃣ From commands → to delegation

**Before:** `Write a function`
**Now:** `I need an email validation module with error logging for a production project`

The agent isn't a robot you give commands to. It's a teammate you brief on the task.

### 2️⃣ From results → to experiments

The first answer is a draft, not a verdict. A bad prompt is data, not failure. Iterate.

### 3️⃣ From actions → to specifications

A prompt is a spec, not a command. A good spec has: **Who** / **What** / **Why** / **How** / **What not to do**.

### 4️⃣ From "do it all yourself" → to attention management

Delegate to the agent: routine, first drafts, search.
Keep for yourself: strategy, final review, ethics.

</Phase>
