<Phase type="activation">

Think of where you lose the most time every week — emails, meetings, content, routine.

Now don't think of it as "tasks." **Picture yourself standing in a hall of mirrors.** Around you: understudies. Each looks like you and knows one slice of your work. One replies to emails in your voice. Another handles notes after calls. A third generates ideas in your style.

Run it in your head: which understudy is already waiting in the wings — ready to go on, the moment you let them?

</Phase>

<Phase type="reflection">

Now try a different frame: **a beehive.** When a hive gets too crowded, the beekeeper splits the colony — part of the bees leave with a queen to build a new home. Each carries an exact copy of the old hive's skills.

Notice in your head: which tasks in your work are ready to swarm — to split off and run on their own? What wants to become its own hive, but you're still keeping everything in one?

**An AI clone isn't a replacement. It's a swarm you send to build the new hive yourself.**

</Phase>

<Phase type="concept">

## Five types of AI clones

| Clone | What it does | Saves |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Communication** | Replies to emails in your voice | 2–4 h/day |
| **Meeting Intelligence** | Summarizes meetings, writes tasks | 30 min/meeting |
| **Content** | Generates posts, clips, ideas | 5–10 h/week |
| **Learning** | Teaches you new things on a schedule | Quality of life |
| **Automation** | Pipeline: data → report, URL → summary | 5–15 h/week |

**Build cycle:** Capture → Build → Test → Refine.

> 💡 Don't build all five at once. Pick the one where you lose the most time. Meetings 5 and 6 show how to do it technically.

</Phase>
